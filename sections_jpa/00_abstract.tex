\begin{abstract}
We study the canonical Fibonacci stabilization map
\[
\Fold_m:\{0,1\}^m\to X_m,
\]
which sends a binary word to the unique admissible Fibonacci word with the same valuation modulo $F_{m+2}$. The local map is many-to-one on isolated windows, but the relevant question is whether this ambiguity survives under sliding overlap. We prove a sharp threshold at $m=3$. For every $m\ge 3$, the overlapping stabilized-window map is injective, admits an explicit finite-memory inverse, and identifies its image with a subshift of finite type topologically conjugate to the full two-shift. At $m=2$, the failure of injectivity is completely concentrated on a unique branch orbit; this yields exact formulas for the conditional entropy and Kullback--Leibler defect and implies mod-zero invertibility off the branch set. On the arithmetic side, stabilization fibers admit an exact residue characterization and a discrete Fourier formula, and their second moment satisfies a linear recurrence. On path space, the conjugacy transports invariant measures, entropy, continuous potentials, pressure, and equilibrium states exactly. In the Sturmian benchmark this also yields deterministic discrepancy bounds for stabilized block laws and for the recovery of finite-range observables. The cut-and-project input is used only to fix the source law and admissible grammar; the theorem-level core is symbolic and arithmetic.
\end{abstract}
