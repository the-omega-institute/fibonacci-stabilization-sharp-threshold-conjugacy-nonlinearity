\section{Beyond Fibonacci: a brief outlook}\label{sec:generalization}

The local arithmetic developed in Sections~\ref{sec:stabilization}--\ref{sec:entropy-ledger} is not tied to Fibonacci numeration. What is specific to the present paper is the sharp sequence-level threshold.

\begin{proposition}[Local numeration template]\label{prop:gen-fiber}
Let $\mathcal A$ be a finite raw alphabet, let $X_m\subset \mathcal A^m$ be a legal language, and suppose that a positional valuation
\[
\Val_m(\omega)=\sum_{k=1}^m w_k\,\omega_k
\]
induces a bijection
\[
\Val_m|_{X_m}:X_m\xrightarrow{\;\sim\;}\{0,1,\ldots,Q_m-1\}
\]
for some modulus $Q_m$. Then
\[
\Fold_m(\omega):=\bigl(\Val_m|_{X_m}\bigr)^{-1}\!\bigl(\Val_m(\omega)\bmod Q_m\bigr)
\]
is well defined, the fibers are residue classes modulo $Q_m$, and the fiber sizes satisfy the Fourier formula
\begin{equation}\label{eq:gen-dft}
d_m(x)=\frac{1}{Q_m}\sum_{j=0}^{Q_m-1}\zeta^{-jr_x}
\prod_{k=1}^m\Bigl(\sum_{a\in\mathcal A}\zeta^{j\,w_k\,a}\Bigr),
\qquad
\zeta=e^{2\pi i/Q_m},
\end{equation}
where $r_x:=\Val_m(x)$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
This is the same residue-class and Fourier-inversion argument as in Sections~\ref{sec:stabilization} and~\ref{sec:fiber-spectrum}.
\end{proof}

Ostrowski systems provide the next natural class of examples \cite{Ostrowski1922,Berthe2001Ostrowski,AlloucheShallit2003}. For the golden mean they reduce to the Fibonacci model. For constant partial quotient $a\ge2$, the local stabilization still makes sense, and already in the silver-mean case the smallest nontrivial window carries a genuine fiber partition.

What is missing beyond Fibonacci is the sequence-level decoder. The Fibonacci threshold proof uses an explicit base-case inversion together with a degree-one overlap argument; no comparable referee-proof decoder is currently available for general Ostrowski systems. For that reason we do not state a uniform analogue of Theorem~\ref{thm:block-invertibility} outside the Fibonacci benchmark.
