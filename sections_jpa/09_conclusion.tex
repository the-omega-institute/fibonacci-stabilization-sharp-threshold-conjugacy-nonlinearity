\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}

We studied the canonical Fibonacci stabilization map as a local folding on binary words and asked when overlap restores the information lost on isolated windows. The answer is exact: $m=3$ is the minimal threshold. Above it, sliding stabilized windows determine the raw trajectory by a finite-memory inverse and identify the image with a subshift of finite type conjugate to the full two-shift.

The exceptional window $m=2$ is fully classified by a unique branch orbit, with exact entropy and Kullback--Leibler defect formulas. This isolates the only genuine obstruction to injectivity and shows that the low-window failure is rigid rather than diffuse.

Once the conjugacy is established, the dynamical consequences are automatic but important: invariant measures, continuous potentials, pressure, and equilibrium states transport exactly. In the Sturmian benchmark the same structure yields explicit discrepancy and boundary-blur bounds for stabilized block laws and finite-range observables.

For the original quasicrystal readout problem, the conclusion is therefore precise. Finite-resolution support inflation makes the full cube unavoidable locally, but above the sharp three-window threshold the ensuing stabilization is not a lossy coarse graining of trajectories. It is a finite-memory recoding.
